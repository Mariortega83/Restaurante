Some Techniques of Us

Sous Vide

A precision cooking method where food is vacuum-sealed and cooked in a temperature-controlled water bath, ensuring even cooking and enhanced flavor.

Searing

A technique that involves cooking food at high heat to create a flavorful, caramelized crust while keeping the inside tender and juicy.

Blanching

Quickly boiling ingredients, then shocking them in ice water to preserve color, texture, and nutrients, commonly used for vegetables.

Emulsification

The process of combining two immiscible liquids, like oil and vinegar, into a smooth and stable mixture, as seen in dressings and sauces.
